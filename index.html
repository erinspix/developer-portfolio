Work

At my alma mater, decorating graduation caps is a cherished tradition. This is my contribution: a meticulously crafted design using acrylic paint on fabric. The cap showcases both creativity and practicality - it's 100% reusable, blending artistic expression with sustainability. It's a unique representation of my academic journey and personal style."
This piece was created in response to a friend's observation that I wasn't painting frequently enough. Inspired by this feedback, I produced this artwork as a gift. It serves as both a personal challenge and a demonstration of my artistic capabilities, addressing the suggestion while showcasing my skills in painting.
This project is a social media graphic that explores the principles of isometric design. Isometric projection is a method of visual representation of three-dimensional objects in two dimensions, where the three coordinate axes appear equally foreshortened. This graphic demonstrates the application of isometric techniques in creating depth and dimension within a flat, digital medium, specifically tailored for social media platforms
This piece represents a practical application of Renaissance sculpture techniques I studied during my time abroad. The work showcases the traditional method of creating a plaster mold from an original clay sculpture. This process, widely used during the Renaissance, allows for the reproduction and preservation of sculptural forms. The plaster mold captures the intricate details of the original clay work, demonstrating the technical skills and historical methods I learned during my international studies
This project represents my final thesis at the College for Creative Studies (CCS), focusing on the creation of procedurally generated textures for 3D environments. The textures were developed using Substance Designer, a specialized software for creating parametric textures. The process involved a combination of node-based and visual programming techniques, allowing for the creation of complex, customizable, and scalable textures. This approach demonstrates advanced skills in technical artistry, combining traditional design principles with cutting-edge digital tools to produce high-quality, adaptable textures suitable for various 3D applications in fields such as game development, architectural visualization, and film

Hi! I'm Erin Spix.

My name is Erin Spix. I graduated from the College for Creative Studies in 2018 with a degree in entertainment-focused art. During my time at CCS, I studied abroad in Italy for two semesters. Currently, I am studying programming through edX at Michigan State University. I serve as the resident millennial and art lead for a California-based startup. I possess a plethora of useless knowledge and have an extensive repertoire of SpongeBob and Vine references.
